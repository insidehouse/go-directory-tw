## 3. TAM / SAM / SOM Analysis

### 3.1 Definitions & Estimation

```
TAM (Total Addressable Market)
= All Taiwan residents who might buy/play board games
= ~5M people (estimated board game aware population)
  × Average spend ~1,500 TWD/year on games
= ~7.5B TWD (~USD 230M)

SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)
= Taiwan internet users who actively search for board game info online
= ~500K people (estimated active online searchers)
  × Average board game purchase 2,000 TWD/year
= ~1B TWD (~USD 30M)

SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)
= Users we can realistically capture in Year 1-2
  via organic search + social sharing
= ~30K monthly visitors × 2% conversion to affiliate click
  × 1,000 TWD average order × 5% commission
= ~180K TWD/month (~USD 5,500/month)
  or ~2.2M TWD/year (~USD 66K/year)
```

### 3.2 Estimation Rationale

**TAM**: Taiwan has 300+ board game stores and 60+ regions with cafes. Party game events regularly attract hundreds. Conservative estimate: 5M people have played or would play board games (21% of population — similar to US penetration).

**SAM**: Active online searchers. Based on keyword research estimates: "桌遊推薦" and related terms likely get 50K-100K monthly searches combined (inferred from mybest and other content sites ranking for these terms). The 500K annual figure captures repeat searchers.

**SOM**: Based on realistic Year 1-2 organic traffic. A well-SEO'd niche site in Taiwan can achieve 30K-50K monthly visits within 12 months (comparable to go-directory.tw growth pattern, scaled for larger market).

## 4. Target User Segments

### 4.1 Primary Segments

| Segment | Size Est. | Behavior | Need |
|---------|-----------|----------|------|
| **New to board games** | ~60% of visitors | Google "桌遊推薦", browse listicles, watch YouTube | "Just tell me what to buy for game night" |
| **Casual players** | ~25% of visitors | Played Catan/Codenames, want something new | "Something like X but different" |
| **Enthusiasts** | ~10% of visitors | Use BGG, know mechanics terminology | "Filter by mechanic, find hidden gems" |
| **Gift buyers** | ~5% of visitors | Seasonal (Christmas, Chinese New Year) | "Best board game gift for [occasion]" |

### 4.2 User Journey Today (Pain Points)

```
Discovery:   Google → mybest article / Cosmopolitan listicle → generic "top 10"
Research:    BGG (English!) → PTT/Dcard search → YouTube review videos
Comparison:  Manual tab switching between 3-4 sources
Purchase:    Shopee / momo / physical store → no clear winner
Post-buy:    "Now what else?" → repeat cycle
```

**Key friction points:**
1. No personalized recommendation (all content is generic lists)
2. BGG data is English → language barrier
3. No single source combines: recommendation + data + purchase links
4. PTT/Dcard posts are unstructured and unsearchable

## 9. Key Takeaways

### The Opportunity is Real

1. **Global market USD 15B+, growing 8-12% annually** — board games are not a dying market
2. **APAC is the fastest-growing region** at 9.5-14.2% CAGR
3. **Taiwan has 300+ stores, 60+ regions with cafes** — demand exists
4. **Zero Chinese-language recommendation tools exist** — clear market gap
5. **21.7M internet users, 90.7% penetration** — the audience is online

### The Product-Market Fit

- **Pain**: "I don't know which board game to buy" — universally felt
- **Solution**: Decision-tree quiz (2-4 questions) + curated directory
- **Differentiation**: Expert logic (from article), Chinese language, Taiwan-specific buy links
- **Monetization**: Affiliate (proven by mybest model) + ads

### Why Now

- Board game market post-COVID growth continues
- Taiwan e-commerce growing 7-8% annually
- Shopee/momo affiliate programs are mature and accessible
- Low competition for Chinese-language board game SEO keywords
- Proven architecture available (fork from go-directory-tw)
